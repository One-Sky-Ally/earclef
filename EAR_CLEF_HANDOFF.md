## 1. What this is

Ear Clef (originally **LeeP**) is Stefano's concept for a values-aligned music platform — "the FIOH before FIOH." Developed extensively around 2023 (documented in a long ChatGPT session, summarized below), then back-burnered because the full vision felt too big to start solo. In July 2026 it was strategically re-scoped: **keep everything except hosting/streaming music**, becoming a one-stop artist hub that aggregates rather than hosts.

The founding insight, in Stefano's words: *there is no ultimate music destination.* To watch an artist's videos you go to YouTube; setlists and tour history, setlist.fm; their story, Wikipedia; tickets, Ticketmaster; merch, their website. Streaming services claim to be the destination but they only hold one piece. Ear Clef merges the fan experience cleanly in one place.

Core values: artist-first economics, genuine fan connection over algorithmic noise, transparency, and releasing good ideas into the world even without profit.

## 2. The original LeeP vision (2023) — full summary

### Purpose
Fix two problems: (1) an industry where most artists can't make a living; (2) no direct, comfortable channel between artists and fans. Plus the one-stop-shop insight above.

### Three account types
- **Artists** — upload any content in any medium; control what's fans-only vs. public; per-member logins with role restrictions; messaging for band/manager/booking.
- **Fans** — free tier streams everything; a music-only social feed (no distractions); playlists, radio, merch, news, events. Paid "super fan" tier per artist.
- **Agents/promoters** — pay for deep performance data: attendance, ticket prices, marketing spend, merch sold, setlists, venue quality, even weather and parking. A data product nobody else offers.

### The membership model (the crown jewel)
- Fans subscribe to an artist for ~$50/year (6 months of guaranteed weekly exclusive content, then **6 months off for the artist** — sustainability built into the business model; a rhythm designed by someone who understands burnout).
- Perk menu: weekly exclusives (demos, b-sides, lessons, behind-the-scenes), early album access, stems for remixing, tablature and how-to-play videos by the writer, 20–30% merch discounts, presales and early entry, live stream + download of shows, fan-made merch with artist approval and profit splits, contests (flyers, remixes, videos) with real prizes.
- Loyalty ladder: multi-year prepays and 3-year rewards (discounts, VIP tickets, signed vinyl).
- **Band bundles**: artists team up as de facto labels — shared subscriptions, cross-promotion, discovery for smaller acts.
- Economics: 5K fans ≈ $250K/yr for a band (≈$40K/member after expenses and taxes for a 4-piece) — a base income floor before tickets, merch, or licensing.

### Platform revenue (sliding scale, artist-protective)
- 0.01%–3% of artist subscription revenue (bigger artists pay a higher %, small artists nearly nothing)
- Licensing marketplace: artists list music for film/TV/games; verified-buyer subscriptions with purchase-history transparency; small residual to platform
- PPV live streams (5–20%)
- Promoter/agent data subscriptions
- "Band boosts": 3 free algorithmic promotions per year for artists meeting engagement criteria
- Platform merch as a support-the-artists movement symbol

### Recommendation algorithm concept
A famously exhaustive dimension list (100+ musical attributes: instrumentation, harmonic relationships, production techniques, lyric devices, recording gear, context of use, and dozens more) to power recommendations beyond genre matching — including *when* users are most receptive to new music.

### From the original roadmap
- Feature priorities (2023 ranking): artist–fan communication and content hosting were "high priority, high complexity" — **note: the 2026 reframe deletes the highest-complexity item (hosting/streaming) entirely.**
- Competitors analyzed: Patreon, Bandcamp, Spotify, Twitch, OnlyFans. LeeP's differentiators: comprehensiveness, loyalty rewards, promoter data, artist-first fee structure.
- Funding paths sketched: bootstrap → crowdfunding → angels; two crowdfunding video scripts written (one populist, one Apple-minimalist).
- The logo was conceived in this chat: yin-yang with treble clef and ear replacing the dots.

## 3. The 2026 strategic reframe (decided July 2026)

**The wall:** hosting music means licensing. Royalty economics are why even Spotify struggles toward profit, and why nobody has merged everything — it's not a lack of imagination, it's rights. The 2023 vision's "free streaming for everyone" is the one unbuildable piece.

**The unlock:** the ultimate music destination doesn't need to host music. **Streaming is the commodity; context is the product.** Precedent: Letterboxd became the cultural home of film without streaming a single movie.

**Ear Clef v-next = the artist-universe hub.** Every artist gets one page that aggregates their whole world: links to the music (Spotify/Apple/Bandcamp), videos, setlists and tour history, upcoming shows and tickets, their story, merch links, community. Pure metadata and links — no licensing, no royalty wall, buildable small.

**What survives from 2023:** the fan-first values, the music-only feed concept, artist-approved fan creativity, the promoter data product (later phase), the 6-on/6-off cadence (as philosophy and manifesto material), band bundles (later phase), the logo.
**What's cut:** hosting audio, streaming, downloads, PPV infrastructure — anything that touches rights.

**Convergence note:** a Vegas-first pilot overlaps naturally with Stefano's Live Now / LVTV concept (live map of Las Vegas entertainment). One project can wear both ideas.

## 6. v1 build plan (for Claude Code)

**Scope discipline is the whole game.** v1 = ONE artist's page, fully working, beautiful. Not "the platform."

**v1 definition of done:** a deployed public page for one artist (Trust in the Sun / Aplete as guinea pig) showing: bio/story · streaming links · embedded/linked videos · upcoming shows · past setlists · merch links · press/news links. Data auto-refreshed where APIs allow, hand-entered where they don't.

**Suggested stack:** Next.js (or similar React framework) · deployed on Netlify (already connected to Stefano's Claude account) · start with a simple JSON/flat-file data layer per artist; add a database only when there are multiple artists.

**Data sources to integrate (register for keys as needed):**
- Spotify Web API — artist metadata, discography, links (free dev tier; quota extension needs approval)
- YouTube Data API — channel/videos
- setlist.fm API — setlists and gig history (free key, easy)
- Ticketmaster Discovery API — upcoming events (free tier)
- Bandsintown — note: their public API is partner-restricted; may need manual entry or alternatives (Songkick API access is also limited) — treat tour data as possibly manual in v1
- Merch/story: manual links in v1

**Phases after v1:** (2) templatize → pages for Boko's circle / Vegas artists, hand-curated; (3) artist self-serve editing + accounts; (4) the music-only feed; (5) promoter data product. Each phase is a separate decision — do not let Claude Code cascade into them uninvited.
